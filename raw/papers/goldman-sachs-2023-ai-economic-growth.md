# The Potentially Large Effects of Artificial Intelligence on Economic Growth
**Authors:** Joseph Briggs, Devesh Kodnani (Goldman Sachs; Jan Hatzius oversight)
**Source:** Goldman Sachs Global Economics Analyst, March 26, 2023

---

## Central Thesis

Generative AI could substitute ~25% of current work globally, equivalent to 300 million full-time jobs. If adopted broadly, it could raise annual US labor productivity growth by ~1.5 percentage points and increase global GDP by 7% over a 10-year period.

## Methodology

- Occupational task data analysis for US and Europe
- Distinction between generative AI capabilities vs. previous narrow/analytical AI
- Three key capabilities of generative AI: (1) generalized use cases, (2) novel human-like output generation, (3) natural language interfaces
- Historical technology transition comparison (MS-DOS to Windows adoption parallel)

## Key Findings

**Labor market exposure:**
- Two-thirds of current jobs exposed to "some degree" of AI automation
- Generative AI could substitute ~25% of current work globally
- 300 million full-time jobs globally at risk of displacement

**Productivity and growth:**
- Potential: +1.5 pp annual labor productivity growth over 10-year post-adoption period
- Global GDP impact: +7% eventually
- Comparison: ChatGPT reached 1 million users in 5 days (fastest adoption ever)

**Technical capability benchmarks:**
- GPT-4 scores 150 SAT points higher than GPT-3.5
- GPT-4: 40% more accurate than GPT-3.5, accepts visual input
- Training compute doubling every ~6 months since 2010s (vs. Moore's Law: ~18 months)

**Historical pattern:** Worker displacement offset by job creation; new occupations account for majority of long-run employment growth

## Key Claims

- Generative AI represents qualitative advance: generalization, generation, accessibility distinguish it from prior automation
- Productivity boom possible but timing unpredictable
- Historical precedent: prior general-purpose technologies caused temporary disruption before growth
- Actual implementation depends on economic/political conditions (not purely technical feasibility)

## Limitations

- "Significant uncertainty" around AI capabilities and actual implementation
- Results depend heavily on difficulty of tasks AI ultimately performs
- Adoption timeline highly uncertain
- Does not model second-order distributional effects (which workers benefit/lose)
- Single institutional view (investment bank research), not peer-reviewed
