## Frame

The document is written as if from mid-2028, looking back at what went wrong. Authors explicitly label it a "scenario, not prediction" representing a left-tail risk scenario to stress-test portfolio assumptions.

## Central Scenario

Continued AI capability advances create a deflationary, employment-destroying reflexive feedback loop: AI increases corporate productivity → headcount cuts → reduced consumer spending → pressure for more automation → further job losses → systemic financial instability by mid-2028.

## Scenario Timeline

### Late 2025–2026: Triggering Events
- Agentic coding tools enable competent developers to replicate mid-market SaaS products in weeks
- Enterprise procurement teams discover in-house development capability; SaaS pricing collapses
- Software companies respond by cutting headcount and investing in AI tools
- Creates reflexive loop: AI adoption → job cuts → funds more AI → enables more job cuts

### 2026–2027: Labor Market Cascade
- White-collar workforce displacement accelerates
- Consumer agents eliminate intermediation rents (travel, insurance, real estate, tax preparation)
- Margins compress toward zero in intermediation-dependent businesses
- Unemployment rises to 10.2% by mid-2028
- Real wage growth collapses despite nominal productivity gains

### Mid-2027: Financial Instability
- "Ghost GDP": output is measured but doesn't circulate through real economy (machines don't buy goods)
- Velocity of money flatlines; consumer economy (70% of GDP) contracts
- Consumer spending decline threatens $13+ trillion mortgage market
- First defaults: PE-backed software companies with bad recurring revenue assumptions

### Late 2027–2028: Systemic Crisis
- Credit defaults propagate across intermediation-dependent business models
- November 2027: market crash
- Deflationary spiral risk; policy response insufficient/too slow
- Public faith in government rescue collapses

## Key Economic Mechanisms

1. **Intelligence Premium Unwind:** Human intelligence derives value from scarcity; machine intelligence at scale disrupts this premise
2. **Reflexivity:** Each company's rational cost-cutting creates collective catastrophe
3. **Friction to Zero:** Agents eliminate intermediation rents that depended on human time constraints
4. **Daisy Chain of Correlated Bets:** Financial system built on white-collar productivity growth assumptions

## Caveats (stated by authors)

- Explicitly labeled scenario, not prediction
- Represents underexplored left-tail risk
- Many mechanisms may not materialize
- Written from February 2026 when S&P near all-time highs — authors acknowledge that's the context
- No existing policy framework designed for world where human intelligence becomes abundantly replicable
