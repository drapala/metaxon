# A Taxonomy of Research Gaps: Identifying and Defining the Seven Research Gaps

## The 7 Types of Research Gaps (Miles, 2017)

### 1. Evidence Gap (Contradictory Evidence)
Findings that challenge widely accepted conclusions. Requires synthesis of existing literature to identify inconsistencies.
**Question template:** "Papers X and Y reach opposite conclusions about Z. What differing assumption explains the divergence?"

### 2. Knowledge Void Gap
A topic or area that has simply not been studied. No existing research addresses the question.
**Question template:** "What has NO ONE studied in this domain? What's absent, not just incomplete?"

### 3. Practical-Knowledge Conflict Gap (Action-Knowledge)
Research findings exist but practitioners do something different. Theory-practice disconnect.
**Question template:** "The research says X works, but practitioners do Y. Why the disconnect?"

### 4. Methodological Gap
Existing research used methods that limit the findings. A different method could yield different insights.
**Question template:** "All studies used method A. What would method B reveal that A cannot?"

### 5. Empirical Gap (Evaluation Void)
Claims exist without sufficient empirical testing. Theory without data.
**Question template:** "This framework sounds compelling. Where's the evidence? What would a test look like?"

### 6. Theoretical Gap (Theory Application Void)
A theory exists but hasn't been applied to a relevant domain.
**Question template:** "Theory T was developed for domain D1. What happens when we apply it to D2?"

### 7. Population Gap
Research exists but only for certain populations/contexts. Other contexts are unstudied.
**Question template:** "This works for X. Does it work for Y? Why might it differ?"
